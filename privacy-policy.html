Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Zen Japanese Massage ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This policy explains how we collect, use, store, and disclose your information.

1. Who We Are

Zen Japanese Massage operates massage therapy studios across NSW, Australia. Our services include remedial massage, facial treatments, and related therapies. Our studios are operated and supervised by an AHPRA-registered physiotherapist.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Contact information: name, email address, phone number
  • Booking information: appointment date, time, location, and service selected
  • Health information: relevant health history, physical concerns, and treatment notes collected as part of the intake and consultation process. This constitutes "sensitive information" under the Privacy Act and is handled with additional care.
  • Payment information: processed via Fresha and HICAPS. We do not store credit card details directly.
  • Private health insurance details: fund name and membership number for rebate processing (where applicable).

3. How We Collect Information

We collect information:

  • Directly from you at the time of booking or consultation
  • Through our online booking platform (Fresha)
  • Through our website via analytics and tracking tools (see Section 7)

4. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information to:

  • Manage and confirm your bookings
  • Provide and improve our services
  • Process private health insurance claims (where applicable)
  • Contact you regarding your appointment or follow-up care
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations

We will not use your information for direct marketing without your consent.

5. Health Information

As a provider of health-related services (including remedial massage), we may collect and handle health information as defined under the Privacy Act. Health information is collected only where necessary for the provision of our services. It is stored securely and is accessible only to relevant staff involved in your care. We handle health information in accordance with Australian Privacy Principle 3 (collection) and Australian Privacy Principle 11 (security).

6. Disclosure of Information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your information with:

  • Fresha (our booking platform) — for appointment management
  • Private health insurers — for rebate claims, with your consent
  • HICAPS — for on-the-spot insurance claims processing
  • Third parties where required by law

7. Website Analytics and Tracking

Our website uses the following third-party tools that may collect data about your visit:

  • Google Analytics (GA4): collects anonymised data on page views, session duration, and traffic sources to help us understand how visitors use our website. Data is processed by Google in accordance with their privacy policy. You may opt out via Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.
  • Meta Pixel (Facebook): may be used to measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns. This tool may link your website visit to your Facebook or Instagram account if you are logged in.
  • Microsoft Clarity: may be used to record anonymised session data (mouse movements, clicks) to improve website usability. No personally identifiable information is recorded.

By using our website, you consent to data collection by these tools. You can manage cookies through your browser settings.

8. Cookies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies for analytics and advertising purposes. You can disable cookies in your browser settings, though this may affect some functionality of the site.

9. Data Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal information from misuse, loss, and unauthorised access. Health information and booking data are stored within secure systems. In the event of a notifiable data breach, we will comply with our obligations under the Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme.

10. Access and Correction

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, and to request corrections if it is inaccurate. To make a request, please contact us using the details below.

11. Complaints

If you have a concern about how we handle your personal information, please contact us in the first instance. If your concern is not resolved, you may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at www.oaic.gov.au.
